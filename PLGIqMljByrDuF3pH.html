What gives a muscle pumping blood, or the fierce pumping?

After a workout, your muscles "inflate" like balloons! Venous pressure also increases. If you have a thin layer of fat in the mirror, on the pump you will see a completely different person with swollen muscles and protruding veins from under the skin! If under the skin is fat, the veins will not be visible, but you will still notice and feel the puffiness and fullness of your muscles. This is a local effect – if you have trained your arms, then your arm inflates, not your legs.

Like this works:

1. your muscle volume is temporarily increased by filling your own blood and osmotic pressure. The muscles become more rigid and inflated, so pumping is often performed before going on stage or before a photo shoot.

2. Blood flows to the area that needs to be nourished and oxygen, that is, to an intensively working muscle group.

3. to create oxygen demand, you will need short pauses between sets (2 minutes or less) and a high number of repetitions (12 or more). If you rest for 5 minutes and perform 5 repetitions, pumping will be minimal.

4. Within 30 minutes after training, the blood leaves your muscles, osmotic pressure returns to normal, and the figure acquires its former modest outlines.

To date science has come to the conclusion that directly pumping does not lead to increase in muscle mass

1. for muscle growth, you need an optimal "mechanical impulse" and a certain "mechanical tension". Such tension occurs when working with a certain optimal working weight before the onset of muscle failure in a range of approximately 5-10 working repetitions and with sufficient rest between sets.

2. Hormones, acidification by hydrogen ions, free creatine, amino acids, filling-pumping blood and other similar factors affect muscle growth in an indirect or secondary way. In other words, these are additional, but not primary, conditions for growth. It is also important to increase working weights, otherwise the mechanical stress will not be optimal and all other processes will no longer matter.

Proponents of pumping believe that with its help, you can still increase muscle growth! They believe that:

1. Filling it will stretch the muscle fascia or muscle sheath, and it is a growth-limiting factor (a dubious theory).

2. Pumping is useful, as the blood flow feeds the muscles and ligaments, which contributes to the healing of injuries (quite likely, but this is not muscle growth, but rehabilitation after injuries).

3. Produces hormones, which grow muscles (wrong because the hormone will not work without optimal mechanical tension, and pumping does not give optimal tension, because it uses insufficient weight and the wrong number of repetitions).

4. Anabolic drugs that professional bodybuilders take, or Pharma, will be better delivered to the muscles by "pumping" (I did not notice this, because when I used them, I did not notice such an effect, in turn, the first 11 years I trained naturally, and the muscles grew without pumping).

Pumping in natural bodybuilding

When I came to the gym and started working out on my own, pumping didn't allow me to build up my muscles, even if only a little. When I switched to weight training 7 years later, I started progressing without any pumping. Therefore, supersets, I consider multi-repetition, short rest between sets and other attributes of pumping to be the enemies of natural bodybuilding.

Occlusive training

It is also calledCURRENT — that is Trening with restriction of blood flow in the muscles. Other names are BFR-training, kaatsu technique-training with tying hands or feet with elastic belts or rubber flagella to stimulate muscle growth.

This method can only be applied to the extremities, chest muscles or you can't work your back that way. In fact, this is an enhanced version of pumping. My friend says that the sensations are simply fantastic and his hands quickly began to grow, despite the fact that he tightens them "on a six-point", according to the ten-point system. However, my friend does not hide that he uses "chemistry", so his recommendation may not suitable for natural athletes.

Results: You you can use the pump at your own risk! However, I wouldn't do that if I were you. My students, who achieved muscle growth using the method I suggested, sometimes tried" for a change " to change from strength training to pumping!

This has happened more than once! But, in the end, they always admitted to their fullest potential frustration: strength indicators and muscle volumes became much smaller, there was a noticeable regression in the first two months of pumping training. No wonder these guys again passed on the "silovogo", vowing more "don't suffer foolishness".
